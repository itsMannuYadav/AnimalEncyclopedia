Insects

Insects are a class of invertebrates within the arthropod phylum. They are the largest group of animals on Earth, representing more than half of all known living organisms. Insects have a chitinous exoskeleton, a three-part body (head, thorax, and abdomen), three pairs of jointed legs, compound eyes, and one pair of antennae.

Key characteristics of insects include:

  • Six legs
  • Three body segments
  • Exoskeleton
  • Many species can fly
  • Undergo metamorphosis

Butterfly

Known for their large, often brightly colored wings and fluttering flight.

Fun fact: Butterflies taste with their feet!

Honeybee

Social insects known for their production of honey and important role in pollination.

Fun fact: A single bee colony can pollinate 300 million flowers each day!

Firefly

Also known as lightning bugs, famous for their ability to produce light.

Fun fact: Firefly light is the most efficient light in the world!

Praying Mantis

Predatory insects known for their distinctive raptorial forelegs.

Fun fact: A praying mantis can rotate its head 180 degrees!

Ladybug

Small, often brightly colored beetles known for eating aphids and other plant pests.

Fun fact: A ladybug can eat up to 5,000 aphids in its lifetime!

Dragonfly

Predatory insects characterized by long bodies, large multifaceted eyes, and two pairs of strong wings.

Fun fact: Dragonflies have been around for 300 million years!
